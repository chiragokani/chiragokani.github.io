Introduction

It might be said that radiation pressure is a phenomenon that the observer thinks he understands—for short intervals, and only every now and then.

R. T. Beyer [1]

These notes were inspired by a lunch discussion with Profs. G. W. Swift, R. Waxler, L. Zhang, A. A. Atchley, J. Mobley, J. D. Maynard, and M. F. Hamilton at the 2024 Physical Acoustics Summer School (PASS).
